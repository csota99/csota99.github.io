NW SPECIES:

Stone

Because they sleep for long periods of time, they tend to collect dirt and plants as a result. Their diet consists mostly of rocks, but also various small animals and the occasional child. For this reason Stone trolls can be very dangerous.

Forest

Forrest trolls are guardians of the forrest. They wander in the night time, and are mostly vegetaeian. The mating call of the forrest troll sounds like falling trees, although if nobody is around to hear it, it doesn't make a sound.

Mountain

Mountain troll are big are the most primal and basic of the troll family. They grow upwards of three hundred feet tall, and move slower than oatmeal. They are mostly calm and docile, but occasionally erupt into firey tantrums.

River

River trolls are the most human friendly of the troll family, unless disturbed by something unatural in or around the river such as polution or excavation. Angry river trolls have been known to swallow up entire groups of river rafters.

Cave

Cave trolls live in caves. Duh. Usually only spotted by splunkers, they are occasionally spotted by miners when the mines intersect natural cave formations, or when the trolls wander into the mines at night and stay for the daytime.
